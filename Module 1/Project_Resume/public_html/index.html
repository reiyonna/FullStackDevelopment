The Dangers of Predictive RAIs in Criminal Justice

By: Reiyonna Stephen

ProPublica, a non-governmental organisation that produces investigative journalism, probed the accuracy of the commercial tool made by Northpointe Inc. (rebranded to Equivant), COMPAS 'Correctional Offender Management Profiling for Alternative Sanctions. It is a software program that gauges a criminal's likelihood of re-offending. The approach to such a Machine Learning Model requires information to be fed into the algorithm, and determine the relevant criminogenic factors to quantify the risk of recidivism. The investigative newsroom compared the predicted recidivism likelihood with the actual numbers synthesising outcomes of presentence investigation cases in Broward County, Florida over a two-year period. It was concluded that these systems, though considered quantitative and persuasive, were inaccurate and racially biased. Inferences showed several instances of false positives and African-American defendants were falsely convicted and/or incarcerated, while their white counterparts were indicated with a lower risk of recidivism.
